First-Line Over-the-Counter Medicines During Pregnancy:   ( Shahd Al Jbouri ) When dealing with common pregnancy pains, it's important to choose "first-line" medications, which are the ones with the best safety records and the most clinical data. Here is a list of safe options in the markets of Jordan, Europe, and the US. 

## 1. Fever and Pain 

- The first ingredient is acetaminophen (paracetamol). 

- Brands that are safe: 

- Jordan: Revanin and Panadol. 

- Panadol and Doliprane in Europe and the UK. 

- Tylenol in the US. 

- Dosage: 500 to 1000 mg every six hours as needed, with a maximum dose of 4000 mg in 

24 hours. 

- Important: Don't take (Extra) versions that have caffeine or NSAIDs like ibuprofen, Advil, etc 

2. Acid Reflux and Heartburn 

- First Line Calcium carbonate, magnesium carbonate, or alginates are some of the 

ingredients. 

- Brands that are safe: 

- Jordan: Rennie and Gaviscon. 

- Rennie and Gaviscon in Europe and the UK. 

- Tums and Mylanta in the US. 

- The "Sodium Note": Gaviscon works very well, but it has sodium in it, so if you have high 

blood pressure or preeclampsia, talk to your doctor. They might want you to use a 

low-sodium option like Tums or Rennie. 

- Dosage: 10 to 20 ml of liquid or 2 tablets after meals and before bed 

3. Morning sickness ,which includes nausea and vomiting 

- The first-line ingredients are vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) and doxylamine (or meclozine). 

- Brands that are safe: 

- Jordan: Navidoxine and Vominore. 

- Xonvea and Cariban in Europeand the UK. 

- In the US, you can buy Diclegis (Rx) or OTCVitaminB6 and Unisom Sleep Tabs. 

- Dose: Usually one tablet before bed 

## 4. Constipation 

- The main ingredient is psyllium husk (fiber) or docusate sodium. 

- Brands that are safe: 

- Agiolax and Normacol in Jordan. 

- Fybogel and DulcoSoft in Europe and the UK. 

- In the US, Metamucil and Colace. 

- Dosage: One sachet or tablespoon in a full glass of water every day 

## 5. Allergies 

- The main ingredient is either loratadine or cetirizine. 

- Brands that are safe: 

- Jordan: Histine and Claritine. 

- Clarityn and Zirtek in Europe and the UK. 

- USA: Claritin and Zyrtec. 

- Dose: 10 mg once a day 

Medications that pregnant women should absolutely avoid ( Masarra Khaleel ) 

There is much talk about pregnant women taking medications, so what are those medications that are prohibited for pregnant women? 

Pregnancy is a sensitive period in every woman's life, accompanied by physical, hormonal, and psychological changes that can significantly impact her lifestyle. This necessitates adopting a new routine to ensure she and her baby receive the best possible care for a safe delivery. Medication is a crucial issue for pregnant women. When you feel unwell, you will think carefully before taking medication, worrying 

about its potential harm to your unborn child and fearing the birth defects and various 

illnesses it might cause in the early stages of development. 

## Methotrexate : 

Inhibits dihydrofolate reductase, blocking DNA synthesis. This disrupts rapidly dividing embryonic cells, leading to miscarriage and malformations. 

## Isotretinoin : 

Alters gene expression via retinoic acid receptors, disrupting craniofacial and CNS 

development. Even minimal exposure is highly teratogenic. 

## Thalidomide : 

Interferes with angiogenesis during limb development, leading to shortened or absent limbs. 

Warfarin : 

Unlike heparin, it crosses the placenta. Its vitamin K antagonism interferes with 

fetal bone and cartilage formation, causing skeletal dysplasia. 

## ACE inhibitors : 

Fetal kidneys depend on angiotensin II; blocking it causes renal agenesis and 

Oligohydramnios 

## Tetracyclines : 

Chelate calcium, depositing in fetal teeth and bones, causing permanent discoloration and 

growth inhibition. 

Valproic acid : 

Inhibits histone deacetylase and folate metabolism, increasing risk of neural tube defects. 

## • Ribavirin : 

Guanosine analog; animal studies show high teratogenicity, so contraindicated in humans. 

## • Statins : 

Cholesterol is vital for steroid hormone and cell membrane synthesis; inhibition disrupts fetal organogenesis 

- Misoprostol : 

Used for medical abortion; if pregnancy continues, exposure linked to Mobius sequence (cranial nerve palsy, limb defects)

Herbs permitted for pregnant women ( Kamal Jalal ) 

## Peppermint : 

Peppermint helps reduce nausea, which most pregnant women experience during the first trimester. Chewing several peppermint leaves in the morning can provide this benefit. Additionally, peppermint helps soothe the stomach and alleviate acid reflux. 

## 2. Ginger : 

Ginger contains plant compounds that help relieve pregnancy symptoms, such as gingerol, which speeds up gastric emptying and reduces morning sickness. Ginger may also help relieve uterine contractions, which most pregnant women experience during the first trimester. 

## 3. Thyme : 

Thyme helps strengthen the immune system of pregnant women. It is rich in vitamins and nutrients beneficial for both the mother and the fetus. It also helps calm the nervous system and improve mood during pregnancy. 

## 4. Chamomile : 

Chamomile is used to relieve gas, soothe the stomach, treat bloating and constipation, and reduce inflammation in the digestive system, providing comfort during pregnancy. Its effects also help alleviate morning sickness 

## 5. Green Tea : 

Green tea offers a range of benefits for pregnant women. It is rich in antioxidants that prevent free radicals from damaging cells. It also speeds up metabolism, which helps combat mood swings during pregnancy. 

Furthermore, it contributes to boosting and strengthening the immune system to fight 

illnesses during pregnancy and alleviates indigestion and other digestive problems that commonly accompany pregnancy. 

## 6. Red Raspberry Leaf : 

These leaves are high in iron and help soothe the uterus, relieve morning sickness, ease labor pains, and promote milk production in breastfeeding mothers. 

## 7. Echinacea : 

Echinacea helps protect against colds and shorten their duration if contracted. 

Hormonal changes during pregnancy can make cold symptoms more severe, requiring natural remedies, which is where echinacea provides relief. 

*Herbs that are prohibited for pregnant women: 

1-Rosemary: Pregnant women are advised to avoid rosemary as it can raise blood pressure. 

It is also difficult for the body to digest, making it hard to break down, and may cause 

fatigue. 

- 2-Dong Quai: Pregnant women should also avoid this herb as it can cause uterine 

contractions, increasing the risk of premature birth or miscarriage. 

- 3-Ginseng: Despite its numerous benefits, ginseng can harm the fetus during pregnancy. 

Therefore, it is one of the most important herbs to avoid during pregnancy. 

- 4-Cohosh: Both black and blue cohosh can cause uterine contractions and premature 

birth. It may also increase the risk of preeclampsia. 

5-Hibiscus: Hibiscus is generally considered healthy for everyone except pregnant women. 

However, it affects hormones and may disrupt the natural hormonal changes associated 

with pregnancy, causing the body to react to the absence of pregnancy, which can affect 

the fetus or lead to premature birth. St. John's Wort: This herb is used to treat depression, 

but it can also cause birth defects or stimulate the uterus, increasing the chances of premature birth. 

- 6-Yarrow: This herb relaxes muscles and is harmful to pregnant women, potentially 

contributing to premature labor. 

7-Lemongrass: It is best to avoid excessive consumption of lemongrass during pregnancy 

as it may increase contractions and lower blood pressure. 

Diabetes and Hypertension in pregnancy : ( Ahmad Munther ) 

## 1. Diabetes (Gestational or Chronic) 

Medication: Insulin 

- Clinical Justification: Insulin is the "Gold Standard" for managing blood sugar during pregnancy. It is a large molecule that does not cross the placenta, ensuring that the medication does not directly affect the fetus. 

* The Risk of Non-Treatment: Uncontrolled blood sugar (Hyperglycemia) poses a greater risk than the medication itself, potentially leading to Macrosomia (excessive birth weight) and delivery complications. 

* Pharmacist's Guidance: Dosage requirements change throughout each trimester. Never adjust your dose or skip injections without direct medical consultation; frequent glucose monitoring is mandatory 

2. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) 

Medications: Methyldopa or Labetalol 

* Clinical Justification: These agents are well-studied and preferred because they effectively manage maternal blood pressure while maintaining stable blood flow to the placenta. 

* The Risk of Non-Treatment: Chronic or gestational hypertension can lead to Preeclampsia (toxemia), placental abruption, or restricted fetal growth if left untreated. 

* Pharmacist's Guidance: These medications are "Conditional" because they require professional monitoring of fetal heart rate and maternal kidney function. Avoid common blood pressure drugs like ACE Inhibitors, as they are strictly prohibited (Red Category). 

Example on appropriate drug : 

Calcium channel blockers (such as nifedipine) : 

Preeclampsia/Eclampsia Risk: CCBs were associated with a reduced risk of preeclampsia or eclampsia 

Birthweight: Unlike Beta-blockers (which were linked to lower birthweight), CCBs had no significant effect on birthweight. 

Doses : 

For Chronic/Non-Emergency Hypertension (Extended Release): 

Initial Dose: 30 mg to 60 mg orally, once daily. 

Titration: The dose can be increased every 7 to 14 days if needed. 

Maximum Dose: Typically up to 120 mg per day. 

Note: This "once daily" regimen improves patient compliance compared to other medications that require multiple doses 

For Acute/Severe Hypertension (Hypertensive Crisis): 

In cases of severe high blood pressure (systolic ≥160 or diastolic ≥110 mmHg), the Immediate-Release form is used: 

Dose: 10 mg to 20 mg orally. 

Repeat: Can be repeated in 20–30 minutes if the target blood pressure is not reached. Maximum: Usually up to 60 mg in a single acute episode.
